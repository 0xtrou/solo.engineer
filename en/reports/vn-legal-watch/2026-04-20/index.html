🔴 HEADLINE: Penal Code Amendment Package Enters Public Consultation — Sweeping Reforms Proposed

The Ministry of Public Security (MPS/Bo Cong An) has released the draft policy dossier for amending the Penal Code (Bo Luat Hinh Su), open for public consultation until May 7, 2026. The National Assembly is expected to review at the 3rd Session of the 16th National Assembly (starting ~May 20) and pass at the 4th Session. This is the most significant criminal law reform package in years.

Key proposals:

1. Death Penalty Reduction — MPS proposes further narrowing crimes eligible for capital punishment, currently at 10 (down from the July 2025 revision). Only "serious crimes" violating "particularly important legal objects" with severe economic/social consequences would retain the death penalty. Specific crimes to be retained or removed are not yet named.

2. Death Penalty → Life Imprisonment After 20 Years — A new statutory provision: if a death sentence is not executed within 20 years (the current statute of limitations under Art. 60), it automatically converts to life imprisonment. Currently, this is only guided by Supreme Court Resolution 03/2025, not codified in law.

3. Double Fines for Financial/Economic Crimes — Fines for crimes in commerce, tax, finance, banking, securities, insurance, and IT/telecom would be doubled. Current minimum fine (1 million VND for individuals, 50 million for commercial entities) is deemed too low relative to modern income levels. Examples: Art. 209 (securities fraud) caps at 2 billion VND; Art. 213 (insurance fraud) caps at 300 million VND — both set to increase significantly.

4. Expanded Use of Fines Instead of Prison — Fines would replace prison for additional categories of less serious, serious, and even "very serious" crimes, provided the offender confesses, cooperates, and fully compensates damages. Rationale: reduce prison overcrowding, recover assets, allow first-time offenders to maintain employment. Precedent: FLC Group case (14 defendants had prison sentences converted to fines at appeal, totaling 2-4 billion VND each).

5. Community Labor as Formal Supplementary Punishment — Currently only a support measure for non-custodial reform (Art. 36), "community labor" would become an official supplementary punishment. Courts could order it regardless of the offender's employment status. MPS cites international models (Germany, Finland, Singapore, Japan).

6. Increased Prison Terms for Socially Sensitive Crimes — Defamation, insult, deceptive advertising, and privacy violations (especially online) face increased prison terms. MPS notes these crimes are increasingly committed by the 16-29 age group.

7. New Crimes and Corporate Criminal Liability — New criminal offenses and behaviors would be added; scope of corporate criminal liability for commercial entities would expand; asset thresholds for criminal prosecution would be adjusted up/down for specific crimes.

🟡 TAX & FISCAL POLICY

Fuel Import Tax Extended to Zero Through June 30

Ministry of Finance proposes extending the 0% MFN import tax on gasoline, diesel, and refinery inputs (naphtha, reformate, condensate) from April 30 to June 30, 2026. Three additional product categories for Nghi Son refinery would also receive 0% rates. Estimated budget revenue loss: 997 billion VND (total since March: 2,021 billion). Context: Middle East conflict has disrupted traditional supply chains from Korea and ASEAN. Even with Hormuz partially reopening, Petrolimex and BSR estimate 5-7 weeks for Middle East infrastructure to restore capacity.

Current prices: RON 95-III at 23,760 VND/liter; diesel at 31,040 VND/liter (up 18% and 61% respectively since the conflict began in late February). Q1 fuel imports: ~3 billion USD, up 78% YoY.

Flexible Tax Threshold for Household Businesses

MoF proposes removing the hard-coded 500 million VND/year threshold for household business tax exemption (PIT + VAT). Instead, the Government would set the threshold flexibly based on economic conditions. Rationale: input costs rising, consumer demand falling, small businesses struggling. The 500M threshold was only set in late 2025. Vietnam has 3-4 million household businesses, 2+ million filing regular tax returns, contributing ~2% of state budget (32,840 billion VND in 2025, +37.5% YoY).

Small Businesses Can Still Use E-Invoices

The General Department of Taxation clarified that household businesses with revenue under 500M VND/year may continue using electronic invoices. Some local tax offices had incorrectly ordered small businesses to stop. The Department instructed local offices to review and withdraw any conflicting notices.

EV Registration Fee Exemption Extended to 2030

MoF proposes extending the 0% registration fee for pure electric vehicles through end of 2030 (vs. current expiry in February 2027). EV registrations have surged: 4,040 in 2022 → 29,281 in 2023 → 79,781 in 2024 → 2.24x more in 2025. EVs save ~0.5 million tons CO2e/year.

Dependent Income Threshold Tripled

MoF proposes raising the income threshold for qualifying as a tax-dependent person from 1 million to 3 million VND/month. The current threshold hasn't changed since 2013 despite inflation and rising living costs. Average per-capita monthly spending reached 2.98 million VND in 2024 (1.6x 2014); average income at 5.15 million/month (2x 2014).
