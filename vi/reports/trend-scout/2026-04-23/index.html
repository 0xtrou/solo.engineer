Conclusions

Top Trends

  1. Agent skills are the new framework layer. Multiple top repos are not apps; they are behavior packs, design files, and methodology for coding agents.
  2. Revenue is strongest where AI touches distribution. Ads, SEO, LinkedIn outreach, social scheduling, video, and conversion tools dominate TrustMRR.
  3. Open-source hype favors infrastructure; MRR favors outcomes. Developers star sandboxes, RAG, context, and agent frameworks; buyers pay for leads, customers, resumes, compliance, and content.
  4. Vertical SaaS is quietly durable. Kibu, TrimRx, medical consulting, and Rezi show specific workflows can outperform broad AI tooling.
  5. Self-hosted remains a strong wedge. Pi-hole, Stirling-PDF, TrendRadar, OpenMetadata: privacy/control attracts attention and can feed hosted/support monetization.

Money vs Hype Gap

  • Hyped but not monetizing: AI skills, CLAUDE.md files, coding-agent taste/design packs, open-source intelligence dashboards.
  • Quietly printing: creator commerce, telehealth, resume SaaS, vertical EHR/compliance, marketing attribution, sales/outreach automation.
  • Best bridge: turn open-source agent excitement into packaged revenue workflows: SEO agents, social agents, codebase context, hosted monitoring, video production.

Opportunities

  • Hosted TrendRadar/worldmonitor for founders/investors: daily competitive intel, Telegram/Slack alerts, saved queries, paid team plans.
  • Agent skill marketplace: curated production-grade skills, evals, versioning, team policy, private libraries.
  • AI coding-context appliance: secure MCP/code search for teams with compliance, audit logs, and IDE/Claude Code integrations.
  • Vertical AI sales agents: DM Champ proves buyers pay for automated closers; clone pattern into dentists, medspas, real estate, legal intake.
  • Self-hosted-to-cloud packaging: Stirling-PDF/Pi-hole-style tools can monetize via managed hosting and business support.

Quick Takes

  • The open-source crowd is building the picks and shovels for agents; the MRR crowd is selling finished outcomes.
  • “Agentic” only monetizes when attached to a painful KPI: leads, conversion, SEO traffic, content throughput, compliance cost.
  • The current gap: tons of starred repos help agents work better, but few give managers a clean ROI dashboard. That’s the product opening.
