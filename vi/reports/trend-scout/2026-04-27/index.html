  • Trendshift: 5+ projects in top 20 (CLAUDE.md, skills dirs, free-tier tools, CLI config, design systems)
  • TrustMRR: SEOBOT ($71k MRR) uses agentic AI for SEO automation; Cometly ($215k MRR) uses AI for marketing analytics
  • Why winning: The "agentic coding" wave is the biggest dev trend of 2026. Tools that let non-technical people command AI agents are printing money while the dev tools themselves get massive star counts. The bridge: skill files and prompt engineering becoming product.

2. AI-Powered Marketing Automation

  • Trendshift: SEO/SEM skills for Claude Code trending; Vibe-Trading (autonomous agents)
  • TrustMRR: PROSP ($128k MRR, LinkedIn AI), Postiz ($97k MRR, 15% growth, social scheduling), SEOBOT ($71k MRR, 9% growth), AEO Engine ($54k MRR)
  • Why winning: AI marketing agents are moving from "nice to have" to table stakes. The ones packaging domain expertise (SEO, LinkedIn, social) into autonomous agents are capturing real MRR.

3. AI Video / Content Generation

  • Trendshift: Hyperbrowser web apps, design systems (visual output), browser harness
  • TrustMRR: Vid.AI ($95k MRR, AI video), Speel ($65k MRR, UGC-style AI video), Supergrow ($79k MRR)

  • Trendshift: Build Your Own X (494.8k stars), System Design (344.6k stars), Codédex

📊 Key Patterns

  1. Agentic coding is the dominant meta-trend. Claude Code skills, Codex skills, browser harnesses, agent runtimes — the entire developer ecosystem is pivoting to "AI agents that code." The trendshift list is >50% agentic coding tools.

  2. GLP-1 digital health is a quiet money printer. TrimRx ($310k MRR, 18% growth) and the medical practice ($150k MRR) show telehealth + GLP-1 is a gold rush that bypasses tech hype entirely.

  3. "For sale" everywhere on TrustMRR. At least 8 of the top 50 startups are marked FOR SALE. Builders are flipping SaaS faster. The market is liquid but maybe frothy.

  4. SEO/LLM visibility is the new SEO. AEO Engine, SEOBOT, SEO Stack, Launch Club — multiple startups focused on "being found by AI" not just Google. This is a new category forming in real-time.

  5. Browser-as-infrastructure for AI agents. 3 separate trending projects (headless browser, browser harness, Hyperbrowser) all solve the same problem: AI agents need to browse the web. Infrastructure layer for the agent economy.

💡 Quick Takes

  1. Build a premium skill pack marketplace. 5+ trending repos are skill/prompt files for Claude Code and Codex. There's clearly demand but zero monetization. A SkillPack SaaS selling curated, tested agent skill files for specific domains (SEO, legal, finance) could capture this.

  2. AI SEO → LLM visibility is the next gold rush. Multiple startups are already doing $50-70k MRR helping businesses rank in AI answers. The first mover who nails "optimize your brand for ChatGPT/Perplexity answers" at scale will win a new category.

  3. The "FOR SALE" signal on TrustMRR is a leading indicator. When 16% of top-earning startups are actively for sale, it means founders are cashing out, not doubling down. Possible market top for indie SaaS — or just rational portfolio management. Worth watching.
