Conclusions

1. Top trends

  • Agent hype is everywhere, but revenue comes from narrow business outcomes. Generic agent frameworks trend; specialized agents for SEO, sales, social scheduling, and ads analytics earn.
  • AI video is becoming infrastructure. Developers want programmable video rendering; customers pay for “idea/script → ready-to-post.”
  • AEO/SEO is a hot commercial wedge. TrustMRR shows multiple revenue businesses around AI/search visibility; Trendshift shows enabling tooling and monitoring interest.
  • Vertical SaaS still quietly wins. Kibu, TrimRx, and medical consulting are not flashy but solve regulated, painful problems.
  • Creator economy remains the biggest money pool. Stan’s $3.57M MRR is the outlier that proves creator monetization infrastructure is still massive.

2. Money vs hype gap

  • Hyped but not monetizing: agent skill packs, prompt/system-prompt collections, design prompt libraries, curated AI resources. These gain stars because they are immediately useful and free, but they need packaging, hosting, workflow management, or enterprise controls to monetize.
  • Quietly printing money: healthcare workflows, creator commerce, resume/career tools, attribution, LinkedIn outreach, and vertical compliance. These products are less exciting to developers but directly connected to revenue, health, jobs, or regulatory burden.

3. Opportunities

  • Hosted “agent skills registry” for teams: permissions, versioning, quality scoring, workflow packs, compliance mappings.
  • AEO/LLM visibility operations suite: monitor mentions across ChatGPT/Perplexity/Google AI Overviews, suggest content/actions, push tasks to agents.
  • Agent-ready video production API: HTML/script input, brand templates, rendered outputs, social publishing hooks.
  • Vertical agent wrappers: take generic open-source agent frameworks and specialize them for dentists, clinics, real estate teams, recruiting, local services.
  • Trend intelligence for operators: combine TrendRadar/worldmonitor-style feeds with business actions: “what should I ship/post/buy/watch today?”

4. Quick takes

  • Don’t build another generic agent framework unless it owns a workflow. The market rewards “agent that sells/books/ranks/posts,” not “agent that agents.”
  • Boring vertical SaaS beats flashy OSS on cash. Kibu and TrimRx have more monetization clarity than most starred repos.
  • AI video + distribution is the near-term wedge. Generation alone is becoming commodity; scheduling, brand control, analytics, and conversion are where margin lives.
