Tóm tắt Điều hành

The 1 May dry run confirms that Vietnam's legal-policy cycle is now dominated by implementation, decentralization and asset/resource mobilization. The headline remains the Government's extension of 0% MFN import duty for selected petroleum products and refinery feedstocks through 30 June 2026, accepting an additional VND 997B budget-revenue cost to contain supply and inflation pressure. New material items from today's fetch are: (i) the Thủ tướng's 464/TTg-CĐS instruction requiring ministries to complete delegated administrative-procedure and business-condition simplification documents by 20 May 2026, with a related Government submission deadline of 5 May 2026; (ii) MOF's draft Quốc hội resolution to accelerate treatment of surplus public land and buildings created by administrative restructuring, including transfer to localities even where legal files are missing or assets are occupied/encroached; (iii) a financial-institution reform narrative under Resolution 66-NQ/TW, including MOF's 2025-2026 legislative output and 4,489 stuck projects worth over VND 3.3 quadrillion; and (iv) growing attention to the valuation of intellectual-property and intangible assets under the 2023 Price Law and Vietnamese valuation standards.

Carry-forward items remain important: Decree 87/2026 advertising sanctions take effect on 15 May; Circular 41/2026/TT-BTC gives crypto-asset tax procedures operational shape; the flexible household/small-business tax threshold implementing decree is still missing; and the 30 June cluster of fuel/tax/fee relief sunsets is now a near-term policy deadline.

1. Petroleum Import-Duty Relief Extended to 30 June 2026

Nguồn: VnExpress, 01/05/2026; Government resolution dated 30/04/2026
Loại: Government resolution extending Decree 72 MFN tariff amendments
Cơ quan ban hành: Chính phủ / Bộ Tài chính implementation
Tình trạng: Enacted — applies through 30/06/2026

Tóm tắt: The Government extended the temporary 0% MFN import-duty regime for selected petroleum products and refinery/feedstock materials for two additional months. Since 9 March, import duties on many petroleum items and inputs such as naphtha, reformate and condensate had been reduced from 7-10% to 0%; the measure would otherwise have expired on 30 April. The new resolution keeps relief in place through 30 June 2026 and adds three products to the preferential list.

Other fuel-tax relief remains in effect: environmental protection tax, special consumption tax and VAT on gasoline, oil and aviation fuel continue at 0% until end-June under the Quốc hội resolution.

Phân tích tác động:

  • Vấn đề được giải quyết: Vietnam is cushioning domestic fuel prices and supply availability amid Middle East conflict risk and disruption to traditional supply channels from South Korea and ASEAN. Petrolimex and BSR warned that even after a conflict ends, regional oil-and-gas infrastructure may require 5-7 weeks to restore capacity.
  • Ai được hưởng lợi: Fuel importers, distributors, transport/logistics firms, aviation, fisheries, manufacturing and consumers via lower inflation pass-through.
  • Thay đổi gì: The emergency tariff window does not close on 30 April; it remains open through 30 June.
  • Tác động tài khóa: MOF estimates the two-month extension reduces budget revenue by about VND 997B, raising cumulative revenue reduction since implementation to VND 2.021T.
  • Yêu cầu tuân thủ: Importers must classify covered goods under the extended tariff schedule and manage contracts around the 30 June sunset.

Tác động kinh doanh: Fuel-sensitive firms should use the relief window for procurement and price planning but prepare for July reversion risk. The policy is framed as emergency stabilization, not a durable tax preference.

2. Thủ tướng Orders Rapid Administrative-Procedure and Business-Condition Simplification

Nguồn: Tạp chí Kinh tế - Tài chính, 01/05/2026; citing 464/TTg-CĐS dated 30/04/2026
Loại: Thủ tướng instruction / digital-government and administrative-reform directive
Cơ quan ban hành: Thủ tướng / Phó Thủ tướng Phạm Thị Thanh Trà signing
Tình trạng: Active directive — near-term deadlines in May 2026

Tóm tắt: The Thủ tướng instructed ministries, ministerial agencies and local governments to urgently implement Government resolutions issued on 29 April 2026 concerning delegation, decentralization, administrative-procedure simplification and business-condition reduction. The instruction operationalizes Conclusion 18-KL/TW dated 02/04/2026 on 2026-2030 socio-economic, fiscal, debt and public-investment planning tied to a future double-digit growth target.

Key deadlines and requirements:

  • Ministries must guide implementation of newly delegated procedures, especially procedures transferred to localities.
  • Ministries must issue implementing amendments within their authority by 20/05/2026.
  • Provincial Ủy ban nhân dâns must receive and operate delegated procedures without service interruption.
  • Ministries including Industry and Trade, Science and Technology, Home Affairs, Public Security, Finance, Culture/Sports/Tourism, Construction and Health must complete simplification plans and send them to the Bộ Tư pháp.
  • The Bộ Tư pháp must consolidate a draft Government resolution by 05/05/2026 covering delegation, conditional-business-line cuts and removal of unnecessary procedures.
  • Ministers and provincial chairs are personally responsible before the Government for results.

Phân tích tác động:

  • Vấn đề được giải quyết: Vietnam's regulatory approvals and conditional-business requirements remain a growth bottleneck. The directive aims to convert high-level simplification commitments into enforceable sector-by-sector changes.
  • Ai được hưởng lợi: Businesses seeking licenses/permits, investors, local governments gaining delegated authority, and citizens using public services.
  • Thay đổi gì: The center is shifting from policy announcements to enforceable implementation deadlines. Localities receive delegated authority but must prevent administrative disruption.
  • Yêu cầu tuân thủ: Regulated businesses should track sector-specific amendments due by 20 May. Companies in conditional sectors should monitor whether their line of business is removed from or simplified under the Law on Investment 2025 Appendix IV regime.

Tác động kinh doanh: This could materially reduce market-entry and operating-permit friction if implementation is real. Investors should watch sectors covered by the listed ministries, especially health, construction, public security, technology and finance, where business-condition reforms can change licensing timelines and compliance costs.

3. Draft Special Mechanism to Unlock Surplus Public Land and Buildings After Administrative Restructuring

Nguồn: Tạp chí Kinh tế - Tài Chính, 01/05/2026
Loại: Draft Quốc hội resolution / special mechanism for public assets and land
Cơ quan ban hành: Bộ Tài chính drafting for competent authorities / Quốc hội consideration
Tình trạng: Draft — consultation and legislative processing ongoing

Tóm tắt: MOF is proposing a special resolution to speed up treatment and exploitation of surplus public buildings and land created by organizational streamlining and administrative-unit restructuring. The draft allows more flexible handling of difficult assets that would otherwise remain idle because of missing legal files, encroachment, mixed residential use or planning inconsistencies.

Key proposed mechanisms:

  • Surplus assets may be transferred to local governments as-is, including cases with missing legal documents, residential use, or encroachment.
  • The ordinary requirement to consult the provincial chair may be bypassed in some transfer cases to shorten procedures.
  • Buildings may be demolished even if still usable or not fully depreciated where the land is converted to public uses such as parks, playgrounds or community facilities.
  • Land-use, construction and specialized planning updates may occur after a competent authority has decided the new use, rather than before.
  • Recipients of allocated or leased land must repay the remaining value of buildings/assets attached to land, including cases where assets were demolished before allocation.

Phân tích tác động:

  • Vấn đề được giải quyết: Public-asset handling is often stalled by missing files, disputed occupancy, depreciation rules and planning sequencing. Administrative restructuring will create many redundant offices and service facilities; without special rules, these assets can become dead capital.
  • Ai được hưởng lợi: Local governments, communities receiving public spaces, investors leasing or receiving converted sites, and the state budget through faster asset monetization.
  • Thay đổi gì: The draft prioritizes speed and practical transfer over perfect file regularization before handover.
  • Yêu cầu tuân thủ: Potential land users must budget for repayment of residual asset value and should conduct careful due diligence on encumbrances, occupancy and planning status.

Tác động kinh doanh: Real-estate developers, PPP investors, logistics operators and social-infrastructure providers should monitor local asset lists after administrative restructuring. However, the same flexibility that accelerates transfer may increase title, valuation and social-clearance risk.

4. Financial-Institution Reform Under Resolution 66-NQ/TW: MOF Shifts From “Management” to “Development Enabler”

Nguồn: Tạp chí Kinh tế - Tài Chính, 01/05/2026
Loại: Policy implementation review / legislative and institutional reform
Cơ quan ban hành: Bộ Tài chính; Resolution 66-NQ/TW implementation context
Tình trạng: Ongoing implementation

Tóm tắt: MOF's implementation review frames financial lawmaking as moving from control-oriented administration toward development-enabling institutions. The article reports substantial legislative output:

  • In 2025, MOF advised issuance of 25 laws/Quốc hội resolutions, 1 Standing Committee resolution, 81 decrees, 5 Government resolutions, 7 Thủ tướng decisions and 144 circulars.
  • In Q1/2026, MOF continued with 22 decrees and 35 circulars.
  • Reform focuses include decentralization, administrative streamlining, international financial centers, the crypto-asset market, digital transformation, science/technology and innovation.
  • As of 31/03/2026, Vietnam had 4,489 difficult/stalled projects with total investment over VND 3.3 quadrillion; 1,022 projects had reportedly been resolved, with further mechanism proposals planned for the remainder.
  • MOF reports 100% of citizen/business recommendations under its review have been handled and publicized.

Phân tích tác động:

  • Vấn đề được giải quyết: Vietnam's growth targets are constrained by legal bottlenecks and stalled capital. The reform program aims to turn legal drafting into a capital-unlocking tool.
  • Ai được hưởng lợi: Investors in delayed projects, businesses affected by licensing burdens, provinces implementing decentralization, and sectors requiring new market frameworks.
  • Thay đổi gì: The emphasis is no longer merely issuing rules, but measuring whether rules unlock projects, capital and new markets.
  • Yêu cầu tuân thủ: Businesses should expect rapid rule changes, more decentralization to local authorities, and new frameworks for emerging sectors such as VIFC and crypto assets.

Tác động kinh doanh: Fast institutional reform creates opportunity but also legal-change risk. Investors in stalled real estate, infrastructure, energy and industrial projects should reassess whether new resolution/decree mechanisms could revive previously frozen assets.

5. Intellectual-Property and Intangible-Asset Valuation Becomes a Policy Priority

Nguồn: Tạp chí Kinh tế - Tài Chính, 01/05/2026
Loại: Policy/legal analysis under the Law on Prices 2023 and valuation standards
Cơ quan ban hành: Bộ Tài chính / Price Management Department context; IP and valuation regulators
Tình trạng: Policy development and professional-standard implementation

Tóm tắt: Vietnam is putting more emphasis on valuation of intellectual property and intangible assets as the economy shifts toward technology, data, brands and innovation. The article stresses the distinction between định giá (price setting/valuation by the state or market) and thẩm định giá (independent professional valuation at a specific time under legally defined standards).

Relevant legal foundation:

  • Law on Prices 2023 provides the core legal basis for valuation and appraisal activities.
  • Vietnamese valuation standards include a standard for intangible assets, covering intellectual property and technology assets.
  • Recognized valuation approaches include market, cost and income methods.

Phân tích tác động:

  • Vấn đề được giải quyết: Technology commercialization, IP-backed financing, capital contribution, M&A and transfer of technology are hampered when parties cannot reliably value patents, software, trademarks, know-how and data.
  • Ai được hưởng lợi: Startups, technology companies, investors, banks, universities/research institutions and state agencies managing innovation assets.
  • Thay đổi gì: IP valuation is moving from ad-hoc negotiation toward professionalized appraisal with legal responsibility.
  • Yêu cầu tuân thủ: Businesses using IP for capital contribution, collateral, transfer pricing, technology transfer or M&A should document legal title, protection scope, commercial exploitation assumptions and valuation methodology.

Tác động kinh doanh: This is a foundational issue for Vietnam's innovation economy. Better IP valuation could unlock financing for asset-light technology companies, but weak data and limited specialist valuers remain a constraint.
